Did You Know? Ducks are highly responsive to each other’s calls—especially in groups. When one duck emits a contact call, nearby ducks often respond in kind, helping the group stay together and coordinate movement. This vocal back-and-forth is crucial for maintaining flock cohesion, particularly in flight or during foraging.
